What is a Cassiopea Jellyfish?

Cassiopea or the upside-down jellyfish, are a genus of true jellyshish and members of the family Cassiopeidae!

What do they look like?

These jellyfish partake in a symbiotic relationship with photosynthetic dinoflagellates and therefore, must lie upside-down in areas with sufficient light penetration to fuel their energy source. When found there may be numerous together with varying shades of white, blue, green and brown.

Where can they be found?

Cassiopea are typically found in shallow lagoons, intertidal sand or mud flats, and around mangroves.
