Types of Clouds

Clouds are classified into several types based on their appearance and altitude. The main types include:

  • Cirrus: High, wispy clouds usually indicating fair weather.
  • Cumulus: Fluffy, white clouds with flat bases, often seen on sunny days.
  • Stratus: Low, gray clouds that cover the sky and may bring light rain or drizzle.
  • Cumulonimbus: Towering clouds associated with thunderstorms and heavy rainfall.

Formation

Clouds form when moist air rises, expands, and cools, causing water vapor to condense into tiny droplets or ice crystals around dust particles in the atmosphere.

Weather Effects

Clouds play a crucial role in weather by influencing temperature, precipitation, and visibility. They can indicate upcoming weather conditions such as rain, storms, or fair weather.
